The Problem

Your most valuable knowledge is trapped in documents nobody reads end-to-end.

Engineering libraries grow for decades. Standards, specs, drawings, procedures ‚Äî thousands of files across dozens of formats. The knowledge that connects them exists only in the heads of senior staff.

Scanned Documents Are Invisible

Field-printed TSBs, faxed contracts, photographed markup sheets. Standard search tools can't read them, so they silently skip the content. You get a confident answer from 70% of your data and never know what was missed.

Drawings Are Treated as Attachments

ISO drawings, P&IDs, electrical one-lines ‚Äî every other system treats these as images to be tagged, not content to be understood. The valve spec on the drawing is just as important as the valve spec in the PDF. Only one of them gets searched.

Revisions Invalidate Old Answers

The client issues a Rev C that changes the check valve spec. Three people already got answers based on Rev B. Nobody knows those answers are wrong. The old document is still in the folder right next to the new one.

Senior Knowledge Walks Out the Door

The chief who's been on the project for 18 months knows which specs apply to which systems, which revisions superseded what, and where the gotchas are. When they leave, that knowledge leaves with them.
